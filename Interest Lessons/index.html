Geography: The Story of Where

Three interactive lessons that turn geography from a list of countries into a way of understanding people, places, nature, culture, and world events. Built for curious kids aged 9 to 12 in Vancouver, Canada — siblings can do it together.

📚 3 lessons ⏱️ 45–60 min each 👧🧒 Ages 9–12 🧩 Quizzes + projects 📍 Local examples

What this course is about

Geography isn't about memorizing capital cities. It's about asking one powerful question — “Where is it, and why there?” — and using it to understand the world. Each lesson stands on its own but they build on each other, ending with a capstone project that ties everything together.

Every lesson includes a plain-language explanation, real examples, a hands-on map activity, discussion questions, a quiz with answers, a take-home mini project, optional challenge questions for advanced learners, and a guide for the parent or teacher.

👧🧒 Two ages, one lesson

Each lesson works for a 12-year-old and a 9-year-old at the same time. The main text is for the older reader; look for the pink “Little Explorer” boxes with simpler words, easier 🧒 discussion questions, and a tap-to-reveal Little Explorer Quiz for the younger one.

How to run it together: read the main explanation aloud, then read the pink box for the little one. Let the older child take the full quiz while the younger does the Little Explorer Quiz — then pair up on the map activity and project (younger draws, older writes).

🏆 Final Review Capstone

At the end of Lesson 3 there's a project — “The Vancouver Geography Portfolio” — that connects all three lessons into one: map a place (Lesson 1), show how its geography shapes people's lives (Lesson 2), and connect it to the wider world (Lesson 3).

Finish all three quizzes and the portfolio to earn the title of Junior Geographer. 🎓

💡 How to use this course

  • Do the lessons in order — each builds on the last, and the final project needs all three.
  • Have Google Maps or a globe handy; the map activities are the best part.
  • Aim for one lesson per sitting. Take the quiz at the end, then do the mini project before the next lesson.
  • Two kids? The older one follows the main lesson; the younger (~9) follows the pink Little Explorer boxes, easier questions, and Little Explorer Quiz — same lesson, together.
  • Parents/teachers: open the “For the grown-ups” panel in each lesson for a short teaching guide, including tips for teaching both ages at once.
