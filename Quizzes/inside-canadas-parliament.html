1 Welcome to Parliament Hill

The Seat of Canadian Democracy

Parliament Hill in Ottawa is the seat of Canadian democracy. Canada's Parliament buildings have served the country for more than 150 years.

The main building, Centre Block, is the permanent home of the Senate, the House of Commons, and the Library of Parliament. Right now it's closed for a big rehabilitation project to restore and modernize it.

🏗️ Parliament is in temporary homes right now! While Centre Block is being fixed up, the House of Commons meets in an enclosed courtyard inside West Block, and the Senate has moved to the Senate of Canada Building on Rideau Street — a former Ottawa train station that is more than 100 years old.
2 Where laws are made

The Two Law-Making Chambers

Canada's Parliament has two law-making chambers. In both, members meet to introduce, debate and vote on bills (proposed laws) and to discuss issues that affect Canadians. Tap each card to learn more.
